# The Stacking

## Chapter 105: The Staying

---

**[ARC 5: THE ORDINARY]**

---

I wake into morning fifty of the ground state and nothing has happened.

I lie in the grey a moment and let that be true, because it is the truest thing there is this morning and I have learned, slowly, over a hundred and four mornings, to trust the mornings when nothing has happened more than the mornings when something has. The new light is still in the middle, in shoal's old warm hole, exactly where it was yesterday — settled, unafraid, warm the way a thing is warm that has never once been cold, and one morning older now, which for a light this new is a whole geologic age and for the marsh is nothing at all, is Monday. It has not moved. It has not fallen. It has not asked anything. It woke, again, into warmth, and found the warmth again to be the whole of what is, and settled, again, into being warm, and that is the entire news of the middle of the marsh on the fiftieth morning: the new light is still here, and still warm, and still nameless, and nothing has happened.

I reach for the stone the way I reach for it every morning, and there it is, STAY at the top in the letters too big to run past, the word that has been the first word on the first stone since before I understood it was the whole of the instruction and not the beginning of one. *The warm underneath* — reed's two words, unchanged, the friend I keep and do not name. The list — reed, sedge, rush, tide, shoal, lark, peat, fen — eight names in the marsh's own hand and no ninth, still, because the new light has been warm for a day and a night and has not needed a name for anything yet, and I do not write one in for it, because you do not name a light before it names itself, and there is no rush, there has never been any rush, the whole finished marsh is the absence of rush made into a place you can stand on. The rope home, in my grip before I am wholly here. I read myself into being the hundred-and-fifth time, and the whole while I read, the marsh does the thing it does on the mornings when nothing has happened, which is: it stays warm. It holds. It is, without me, without anyone doing anything, simply and entirely still here.
